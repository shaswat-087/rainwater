Groundwater Recharge System

🌿 Managing Excess Water in Urban Areas 🌿

Why Groundwater Recharge?

In areas with large rooftop surfaces or high rainfall, surplus water often goes to waste. A groundwater recharge system helps to harvest this excess water, directing it into recharge pits or bore wells. This process replenishes underground aquifers, ensures sustainable water supply, and reduces the risk of urban flooding.

⚙️ Step-by-Step Building Groundwater Recharge System 🛠️

1.Assess the Area

🔷 Identify areas with large rooftops or heavy rainfall.

🔷 Check soil type and water absorption capacity.

2. Collect Rainwater

🔷 Install gutters and pipes on rooftops to collect rainwater.

🔷 Ensure proper slope so water flows easily.

3. Install a Filtration Unit

🔷 Use mesh filters to remove leaves and debris.

🔷 Add layers of sand, gravel, and charcoal for better filtration.

4. Construct a Recharge Pit

🔷 Dig a pit (1–3 meters deep).

🔷 Fill it with layers.

  • Bottom → large stones
  • Middle → gravel
  • Top → sand

5. Connect Pipes to the Pit

🔷 Direct filtered rainwater into the recharge pit or borewell using pipes.

6. Allow Water Percolation

🔷 Water slowly seeps into the ground and recharges underground aquifers.

7. Regular Maintenance

🔷 Clean filters and pipes periodically.

🔷 Remove blockages before rainy season.

🔷 Inspect the pit for proper functioning.
